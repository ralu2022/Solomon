Solomon's Stones



Solomon's Stones make up an area of tourist interest, located in the southwestern part of the Șchei district of Brașov, Transylvania, Romania.

~ Where is located ~

Solomon's stones are located in the northwestern part of the Postavaru Massif, between Mount Tampa and the Stejerișul Mare nature reserve, being crossed by the Valea cu Apa river (known by the locals as the Solomon river), a tributary of the Șcheiu river.
~ History ~

Legend has it that the name of these rocks comes from a Hungarian king, Solomon, who in his flight of pursuers, would have jumped with his horse over the valley that crosses the two rocks, the king escaping with his life, and his enemies falling into the abyss [1]. Julius Teutsch, a Saxon chronicler (the initiator of the founding of the Saxon Museum of the Barsa Country [2]), who wrote the Ethnography of the Barsa Country, discovered at the beginning of the twentieth century (in 1913) an ancient fortification (Dacian fortress) upstream of the Valley with Water, in the place known by the people of Schei as “Between Chietri”, a place where, from time immemorial, the Juniors from the Scheii of Brașov climbed on Easter Wednesday to perform the ritual of throwing the mace and playing the dance of the juniors. Today, this traditional event of the Scheians is known as "The Descent of the Juniors into the Citadel and the Ascension to the Stones of Solomon" being carried out by the Union of Juniors from the Scheii of Brașov and Brașov Vechi. in partnership with the City Hall of Brașov. The day on which this event was set is always the first Sunday after Easter Sunday, known as Tomii Sunday.
